PlantRight.org

PlantRight.org is a San Francisco, CA based non-profit organization. They focus on raising awareness about the problem of invasive botanical species in the state.

PlantRight.org was originally a custom built CMS, written in PHP. There is a rather extensive database-backed feature that will display the list of species that are considered invasive for each planting zone in the state. Which plants are displayed in the list depends on the regions of the state selected. New plants are expected to be added to the list, and making it easy to add relevant news about invasive species was an obvious feature. Once the original developer moved on, there was nobody left in the organization who knew how to update the website.

They have a future roadmap including a new section to catalogue and rank potential invasive species to be added to the list which sounded like a perfect use case for a Drupal implementation. Much of the heavy-lifting of sorting and categorizing the plant species is easily accomplished with the CCK and Views modules. There is also Imagefield/Imagecache and Lightbox integration on the individual species pages to give the user a more polished experience when viewing. YUI editor is used for content editing.

Project Details:

  • Drupal, Fusion-based theme
  • Lots of Views Arguments
  • Community features for CA Master Gardeners
  • Lots of searchable content, Lucene coming soon
  • TypeKit fonts everywhere!
